Section 1 Reference

Comparable playbooks.
Not Calm & Headspace. Oura, WHOOP, Function, AG1.

Oura
Scientific validation as inflection

Sold ~150K rings in first 5 years. 2017 Stanford study vs. polysomnography unlocked the press cycle + celebrity adoption (Prince Harry, Ronaldo).

DUST analog: MIT research + Adam's PhD. Publish — even small-N — in peer-reviewed venues. Convert to press hooks.

WHOOP
Aspirational community as free distribution

Four years (2012–2016) closed to elite athletes only. NFLPA deal 2017 distributed to every NFL player. Zero-CAC growth.

DUST analog: Close cohort to 200–500 "Founding Dreamers" — lucid YouTubers, dream researchers, Yoga Nidra teachers, biohacker podcasters.

Function Health
Founder authority + DTC speed

$298M Series B in Nov 2025, $2.5B valuation on $100M run-rate. 450% YoY 2024→2025. Engine: Mark Hyman's pre-existing authority + podcast audience.

DUST analog: Adam's media presence is a primary growth asset, not a side activity.

AG1
Podcast + creator compounding

$403K → $78.9M earned media value (2018→2023) — 19,470% increase. Deep affiliate model with Ferriss, Shepard, Rogan, Huberman.

DUST analog: Huberman, Rich Roll, Sam Harris, Aubrey Marcus, Tim Ferriss, Andrew Holecek, Charlie Morley — a natural starter set.

Calm & Headspace
The wrong reference for early growth

Calm's inflection: Sleep Stories 2017 — five years after launch. Headspace: Puddicombe's pre-existing media 2010–2014 before the app existed.

Why it's wrong: Both had already crossed PMF. Headspace subs declined 300K in 2025. These are retention plays, not growth plays.

Eight Sleep
Hardware unit economics discipline

$50M raised in 2026 at $1.5B valuation on $500M+ lifetime Pod sales. Free-cash-flow positive in 2025.

DUST analog: Design subscription unit economics that work without paid acquisition, before scaling.

Shape / Prophetic AI
Niche-but-loyal + direct competitor

Shape (lucid dreaming app, $39.99/yr): 94% satisfaction, 1,700+ five-star reviews. Validates WTP but caps below mass scale.

Prophetic AI: $1.1M raised, pre-revenue. Most direct competitor in headband-form-factor dream tech. Market will be there in 2027.

Waking Up (Sam Harris)
Cleanest playbook to copy

Launched to pre-existing audience, designed for retention and depth rather than scale, charged subscription from day one.

DUST analog: BBC + MIT + 100M earned impressions is similar fuel.

Section 4 Hypothesis

Risks, open questions & caveats.
What could invalidate the plan.

HIGH
Regulatory exposure on clinical claims
Marketing to nightmare, PTSD, grief, insomnia segments with disease-specific language risks FDA classification and FTC scrutiny. Mitigation: "sleep wellness" framing, no "treatment" or "cure," qualified disclaimers. Keep clinical segments earned-only.
MED
$585B figure is not directly sourced
Pivot deck to defensible aggregate construction (~$430B) with category list. Numerical imprecision in an investor pitch is a credibility risk.
HIGH
Hardware development timeline
Comparable hardware (Oura, Muse, Apollo Neuro, Lumenate, Prophetic) all moved slower than software. Price in 12–18 months of delay risk, not the optimistic plan.
MED
CAC inflation is real & structural
Apple privacy (2024) + Meta health-category restrictions (2025) raised CAC 30–50%. The plan should not depend on cheap paid social — community + podcast + earned media is the compensating play.
LOW
"$0 paid" assumption may be overcorrected
Apple Search Ads ($3–7 CPI) and Reddit promoted posts in relevant subreddits remain efficient. Principle is "earned and community first," not "no paid ever."
MED
Competitive entry timing
Prophetic's headband in development; consciousness-tech entrants emerging quarterly. DUST's clearly differentiated brand window is 18–24 months.
HIGH
Cultural & ethical exposure
Religious-cultural and grief segments are right to win in time, wrong to rush. A misstep on Indigenous, Muslim, or grief communities can permanently damage the brand. Earned + partnership only.
MED
Founder bandwidth
Function, Headspace, Waking Up all depend on founder-as-content-asset. Book Adam's recurring media slots (TED talk, 2 podcasts/month, 1 written piece/quarter) as non-negotiable inputs.
